Papers

Electric Vehicle (EV) range prediction using Machine Learning, specifically Linear Regression, involves estimating how far an EV can travel on a single charge by analyzing key factors such as battery capacity, speed, temperature, driving behavior, and road conditions. The model learns the relationship between these input variables and the output (range) using historical data, enabling it to make accurate predictions for new inputs. This approach helps address range anxiety by providing reliable distance estimates, improves route planning, and supports efficient battery usage. The outcome of this model is a reasonably accurate and fast prediction system that enhances user decision-making, optimizes energy efficiency, and demonstrates the practical application of AI in intelligent transportation systems.

This paper proposes a Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) framework for accurate forecasting of hybrid solar-wind power generation. Unlike traditional machine learning models, the proposed approach integrates physical laws such as the Beer-Lambert law for solar radiation, cubic wind power relationships, and energy conservation principles into the neural network training process. This ensures that predictions are not only accurate but also physically consistent. The model was tested using real-world data from a 100 MW hybrid power plant in Tamil Nadu and achieved improved accuracy compared to conventional methods. Additionally, the framework incorporates uncertainty quantification techniques to provide reliable prediction intervals. The results demonstrate that PINNs significantly reduce unrealistic predictions, enhance generalization under varying conditions, and offer a robust solution for renewable energy forecasting and grid management.
